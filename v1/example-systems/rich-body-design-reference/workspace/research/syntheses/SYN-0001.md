# Prompt Chain Repair Strategies For Long-Running Agents

This synthesis was commissioned after several incident reviews converged on the same failure mode: when an agent drifts off the intended instruction chain, operators can often see the problem but do not know the least-destructive repair move.

The goal is not to define a universal repair policy. The goal is to identify what kinds of interventions preserve task continuity without hiding the original failure.

## QUESTION

What intervention pattern best restores a long-running agent after prompt-chain drift without destroying the evidence needed for later diagnosis?

### Decision Pressure

- Full resets are reliable but expensive.
- Silent in-place edits recover quickly but can erase the trail that explains the drift.
- Operators need a repeatable middle path.

## EVIDENCE

### Source: Incident Review Notes

> Operators recovered faster when they inserted an explicit repair instruction as a new turn instead of rewriting prior turns in place.

### Source: Runbook Feedback

> The safest repair move was the one that left the broken plan visible and clearly marked the override boundary.

### Source: Replay Trace

The best-performing traces shared two traits:

- they preserved the failed chain for later inspection
- they introduced a short repair turn that named the exact violated constraint

```text
repair turn template:
"Pause the previous plan. Restate the active constraint in one sentence. Produce the next step only if it satisfies that constraint."
```

### Contradictory Signal

> In very small tasks, a full reset was sometimes cheaper than a repair turn because the agent had so little state worth preserving.

## SYNTHESIS

### Pattern That Repeats

Repair works best when it is explicit, additive, and narrow.

1. Explicit: the intervention names the broken constraint.
2. Additive: the original failure path remains visible.
3. Narrow: the repair constrains only the next step instead of restating the whole system prompt.

### Intervention Matrix

| Intervention | Preserves failure trail | Recovery speed | Operational risk | Practical note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Full reset | Low | Medium | Low | Reliable, but it discards the drift context operators later want to inspect. |
| Silent in-place rewrite | None | High | High | Fastest apparent recovery, but it erases the very evidence that explains the failure. |
| Additive repair turn | High | High | Medium | Keeps the failed chain visible while constraining the next step tightly enough to recover. |

### Boundary Condition

A full reset is still preferable when:

- the task state is tiny
- the agent has already compounded multiple contradictory repairs
- operators cannot reliably name the violated constraint

## DECISION

Adopt additive repair turns as the default first response for prompt-chain drift in long-running agent tasks.

### Constraints

- Do not rewrite earlier turns in place.
- Preserve the failed chain for review.
- Escalate to full reset when repair turns accumulate without restoring stable behavior.

## OPEN_QUESTIONS

- How many additive repairs should be allowed before the system recommends a reset automatically?
- Should repair turns carry a standardized marker for downstream analysis tooling?
